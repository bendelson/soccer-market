The global soccer inflation

An analysis on the market for male soccer players in the last twenty years. By Ben Mendelson

Here comes an anecdote about the soccer world and how it has changed with big money coming in.

Do you still remember Thierry Henry?

The French national was a striker for Arsenal London and pretty succesful. Back in his prime, twenty years ago, Henry was worth 45 million euros.

That meant: He was the most valuable player in the English Premier League. Each bag of money in this picture represents five million euros.

Now let me introduce you to a Norwegian with long blond hair. Erling Haaland is the current Premier League player with the highest market value. Since 2022, he plays for Manchester City.

Bayern Munich once tried to hire him, but they joked about having to send him to a hairdresser first.

Oh, by the way, his market value is estimated at 180 million euros.

So that would mean, he is now worth four Henrys.

Before taxes and inflation, of course.

Haaland's team mates from Manchester City on average are worth 38.2 million euros today.

Twenty years ago, the average player of ManCity was worth 2.8 million euros.

So today, the players are worth fourteen times the amount from twenty years ago.

Arguably, players today probably aren't fourteen times as good as they were twenty years ago. The increase rather has to do with the involvement of big investors and the growth of soccer economy overall.
